BEST BICEPS WORKOUTS

Barbell Bicep Curl

The barbell bicep curl is a classic exercise for building bicep strength and size. To perform this exercise, stand with your feet shoulder-width apart and hold a barbell with an underhand grip. Curl the barbell up toward your chest, keeping your elbows close to your body. Lower the barbell back down and repeat for several reps.

Dumbbell Hammer Curl

The dumbbell hammer curl is a variation of the bicep curl that targets both the biceps and forearms. To perform this exercise, hold a dumbbell in each hand with a neutral grip. Curl the dumbbells up toward your chest, keeping your elbows close to your body. Lower the dumbbells back down and repeat for several reps.

Cable Curl

The cable curl is an excellent exercise for targeting the biceps using a cable machine. To perform this exercise, attach a straight bar to the low pulley of a cable machine. Stand facing the machine, grab the bar with an underhand grip, and curl it up toward your chest. Slowly lower the bar back down and repeat for several reps.

Incline Dumbbell Curl

The incline dumbbell curl is performed on an inclined bench and targets the biceps from a different angle. To perform this exercise, sit on an incline bench with a dumbbell in each hand. Let your arms hang straight down and curl the dumbbells up, squeezing your biceps at the top. Lower the dumbbells back down and repeat for several reps.

Concentration Curl

The concentration curl is an isolation exercise that focuses on one arm at a time. Sit on a bench with your legs spread apart, hold a dumbbell in one hand, and place your elbow on the inside of your thigh. Curl the dumbbell up, focusing on your biceps contraction. Lower it back down and repeat for several reps before switching arms.

Dumbbell Preacher Curl

The dumbbell preacher curl is performed on a preacher bench and isolates the biceps. Sit on the preacher bench, hold a dumbbell in one hand, and curl it up, keeping your upper arm firmly against the bench. Lower the dumbbell back down and repeat for several reps before switching arms.

Cable Rope Hammer Curl

The cable rope hammer curl is performed using a rope attachment on a cable machine. Stand facing the machine, grab the rope with a neutral grip, and curl it up toward your chest. Slowly lower the rope back down and repeat for several reps.

Decline Dumbbell Curl

The decline dumbbell curl is performed on a decline bench and targets the biceps differently. Lie on a decline bench, hold a dumbbell in each hand, and curl them up, squeezing your biceps. Lower the dumbbells back down and repeat for several reps.

Zottman Curl

The Zottman curl is a versatile biceps exercise that also targets the forearms. Hold a dumbbell in each hand with an underhand grip, curl them up, and then rotate your wrists to an overhand grip at the top. Slowly lower the dumbbells back down in the reverse grip and repeat for several reps.

Ez-bar Curl

The Ez-bar curl is performed using an Ez-bar, which can be easier on the wrists. Hold the Ez-bar with an underhand grip, curl it up toward your chest, and slowly lower it back down. Repeat for several reps.

Spider Curl

The spider curl is performed on an incline bench with your chest supported. Hold a barbell with an underhand grip, curl it up toward your chest, and then lower it back down. Repeat for several reps.
