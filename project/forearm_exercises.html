BEST FOREARM WORKOUTS

Wrist Curls

Wrist curls are a forearm exercise that target the muscles of the forearm flexors. To perform this exercise, sit on a bench, rest your forearms on your thighs, and hold a dumbbell in each hand with your palms facing up. Curl your wrists upward, lifting the dumbbells, and then lower them back down. Repeat for several reps.

Reverse Wrist Curls

Reverse wrist curls target the forearm extensors. Sit on a bench, rest your forearms on your thighs, and hold a dumbbell in each hand with your palms facing down. Curl your wrists upward, lifting the dumbbells, and then lower them back down. Repeat for several reps.

Farmer's Walk

The farmer's walk is a functional exercise that works the entire forearm. Hold a heavy dumbbell in each hand, stand tall, and walk for a certain distance or time. The weight challenges your grip and forearm muscles. Repeat for several sets.

Plate Pinch

Plate pinch involves holding weight plates with your fingertips and thumb, pinching them together. Stand or sit and pinch two weight plates together, lifting them off the ground. Hold for a few seconds and then lower them back down. Repeat for several reps.

Wrist Roller

The wrist roller is a forearm exercise that requires a wrist roller device. Attach a weight to the wrist roller, hold it at arms' length in front of you, and roll it up and down by winding and unwinding the cord. Repeat for several reps in both directions.

Zottman Curl

The Zottman curl is a versatile biceps exercise that also targets the forearms. Hold a dumbbell in each hand with an underhand grip, curl them up, and then rotate your wrists to an overhand grip at the top. Slowly lower the dumbbells back down in the reverse grip and repeat for several reps.
