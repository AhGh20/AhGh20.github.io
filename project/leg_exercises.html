BEST LEGS WORKOUTS

Barbell Back Squat

This exercise is a compound movement that primarily targets the quadriceps, hamstrings, and glutes. To perform this exercise, stand with your feet shoulder-width apart, with a barbell resting on your upper back. Bend at the knees and hips to lower yourself down until your thighs are parallel to the ground. Drive back up through your heels to return to the starting position.

Split Squat

This exercise is a unilateral movement that targets the quadriceps, hamstrings, and glutes. To perform this exercise, stand with one foot forward and one foot back, with your feet hip-width apart. Lower yourself down until your back knee is just above the ground, then drive back up through your front heel to return to the starting position.

Lunge

This exercise is a unilateral movement that targets the quadriceps, hamstrings, and glutes. To perform this exercise, take a big step forward with one foot, then lower your body down until your front thigh is parallel to the ground and your back knee is just above the ground. Drive back up through your front heel to return to the starting position, then repeat with the other leg.

Leg Press

This exercise is a machine-based exercise that primarily targets the quadriceps, but also works the hamstrings and glutes. To perform this exercise, sit in the leg press machine with your feet on the platform. Push the platform away from you until your legs are fully extended, then lower the platform back down until your knees are at a 90-degree angle.

Leg Extensions

This exercise primarily targets the quadriceps. To perform this exercise, sit in a leg extension machine with your feet under the padded bar. Lift the bar by straightening your legs until your legs are fully extended, then lower the bar back down until your knees are at a 90-degree angle.

Leg Curl

This exercise primarily targets the hamstrings. To perform this exercise, lie face down on a leg curl machine with your heels hooked under the padded bar. Curl your legs up towards your buttocks, then lower them back down.

Standing Calf Raise

This exercise primarily targets the gastrocnemius muscle in the calves. To perform this exercise, stand on the edge of a step or raised surface with your heels hanging off the edge. Push up onto your toes as high as possible, then lower back down until your heels are below the step.

Seated Calf Raise

This exercise primarily targets the soleus muscle in the calves. To perform this exercise, sit in a calf raise machine with your feet under the padded bar. Push up onto your toes as high as possible, then lower back down until your heels are below the platform.
