BEST TRICEPS WORKOUTS

Tricep Dips

Tricep dips are a classic bodyweight exercise for targeting the triceps. To perform this exercise, find parallel bars or use a sturdy surface like parallel bars or the edge of a bench. Lower your body down by bending your elbows and then push back up. Repeat for several reps.

Tricep Pushdown

The tricep pushdown is a cable machine exercise that effectively isolates the triceps. Attach a straight bar to the high pulley of a cable machine. Stand facing the machine, grab the bar with an overhand grip, and push it down until your arms are fully extended. Slowly release the bar back up and repeat for several reps.

Diamond Push-Ups

Diamond push-ups are a variation of the traditional push-up that places more emphasis on the triceps. Get into a push-up position with your hands close together, forming a diamond shape with your thumbs and index fingers. Lower your body down, keeping your elbows close to your sides, and push back up. Repeat for several reps.

Close-Grip Bench Press

The close-grip bench press is a variation of the bench press that targets the triceps. Lie on a flat bench and grip the barbell with your hands closer together than shoulder-width apart. Lower the barbell to your chest and press it back up. Repeat for several reps.

Tricep Kickbacks

Tricep kickbacks are a dumbbell exercise that isolates the triceps. Hold a dumbbell in one hand, bend your knees, hinge at the hips, and lean forward slightly. Extend your arm back, keeping it close to your body, and squeeze your triceps at the top. Lower the dumbbell back down and repeat for several reps before switching arms.

Lying Triceps Extension

Lying triceps extensions, also known as skull crushers, target the triceps effectively. Lie on a bench with a barbell or dumbbells, extend your arms upward, and lower the weight behind your head. Extend your arms back up to the starting position and repeat for several reps.

Overhead Triceps Extension

The overhead triceps extension is performed by holding a dumbbell or barbell overhead with both hands and extending your arms upward. Lower the weight behind your head, keeping your elbows close to your head, and then extend your arms back up. Repeat for several reps.

Triceps Push-Down

Triceps push-down is an isolation exercise using a cable machine. Attach a rope attachment to the high pulley, grasp it with both hands, and push the rope down until your arms are fully extended. Slowly release the rope back up and repeat for several reps.

Cable Rope Overhead Triceps Extension

The cable rope overhead triceps extension targets the triceps from a different angle. Stand facing away from the cable machine, hold the rope attachment with both hands overhead, and extend your arms upward. Lower the rope behind your head and then extend your arms back up. Repeat for several reps.

Dumbbell Skull Crusher

The dumbbell skull crusher is another effective triceps exercise. Lie on a bench with dumbbells in hand, extend your arms upward, and lower the dumbbells toward your forehead, keeping your elbows stationary. Extend your arms back up and repeat for several reps.
